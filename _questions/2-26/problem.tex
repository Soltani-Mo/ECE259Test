\problem{\textit{Capacitors with a nonlinear dielectric}. Consider an arbitrary shaped capacitor with a nonlinear dielectric. The electric field intensity, electric flux density, and polarization vectors in the dielectric are $\vec{E}$, $\vec{D}$, and $\vec{P}$, respectively. At any point in the dielectric and for any field intensity, the following vectors are linearly proportional to $\vec{E}$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]\setlength{\parskip}{1pt}
\item $\vec{D}$ only.
\item $\vec{P}$ only.
\item Both $\vec{D}$ and $\vec{P}$.
\item $\vec{D} - \vec{P}$.
\item None of the above.
\end{enumerate}}

\solution{(D)}
\answer{(D)}
