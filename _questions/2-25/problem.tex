\problem{\textit{Hundred capacitors connected in series/parallel}. We have a set of $100$ capacitors of arbitrary geometries, with different capacitances $C_1, C_2, ..., C_{100}$. Let $C_\text{series}$ and $C_\text{parallel}$ be the equivalent total capacitances of the capacitors connected in series [Fig.~Q2.11(a)] and parallel [Fig.~Q2.11(b)], respectively. Comparing these two equivalent capacitances, we have
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]\setlength{\parskip}{1pt}
\item {$C_\text{series} < C_\text{parallel}$.}
\item $C_\text{series} = C_\text{parallel}$.
\item $C_\text{series} > C_\text{parallel}$.
\item Depends on the geometries of the individual capacitors and nothing else.
\item Depends on the relationship between particular values of $C_1, C_2, ..., C_{100}$.
\end{enumerate}
