\problem{\textit{Electric potential at a point in a uniform electric field.}
Consider a region with a unform electrostatic field of intensity~$E$, as shown in FIg.Q1.6. If the electric scalar potential at the point A is zero ($V_A = 0$), the
potential at the point B, given the notation in the figure, equals
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Alph*)]\setlength{\parskip}{1pt}
\item $V_B = Ed$.
\item $V_B = Ed\sin \alpha$.
\item $V_B = -Ed\sin \alpha$.
\item $V_B = Ed\cos \alpha$.
\item $V_B = -Ed\cos \alpha$.
\item $V_B = 0$.
\end{enumerate}
