\problem{[Cheng 6-42] Calculate the force per unit length on each of three equidistant, infinitely long, parallel wires $0.15$ (m) apart, each carrying a current of $25$ (A) in the same direction. Specify the direction of the force.}

\solution{The three wires are shown in the diagram below.
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[scale=0.4]{\ppath Cheng6-42_diagram.png}
\end{center}
The three currents in the wires are equal $I_1=I_2=I_3=25\,\text{(A)}$, while the distance between them is $d=0.15\,\text{(m)}$. 

The magnetic flux density at wire 2 is
\begin{align*}
\vect{B}_2 &= \uvect{a}_x2{B}_{12}\cos 30^\circ \\
&= \uvect{a}_x\frac{\sqrt{3}\mu_0 I}{2\pi d}.
\end{align*}
Force per unit length on wire 2 is 
\begin{align*}
\vect{f}_2 &= -\uvect{a}_y I B_2\\
&= -\uvect{a}_y \frac{\sqrt{3}\mu_0 I^2}{2\pi d}\\
&= -\uvect{a}_y 1150\mu_0\\
&= -\uvect{a}_y 1.44\times 10^{-3}\, \text{(N/m)}.
\end{align*}
The forces on all three wires are equal in magnitude and all point towards the center of the triangle.
}


\answer{ The force on one of the wires is
\begin{align*}
\vect{F}=-a_y 1.44\,\times\,10^{-3}\, \text{(N/m)}.
\end{align*}
The forces on all three wires are equal in magnitude and all point towards the center of the triangle.
}
